The History of

Panglao Bohol

Panglao, an island nestled southwest of Bohol in the Philippines, boasts a rich history dating back centuries. Its story begins with the arrival of Austronesian peoples, skilled seafarers who established settlements along its shores, drawn by abundant marine resources. Over time, Panglao became a hub for trade, welcoming Chinese merchants and embracing Islamic influences. The Spanish colonization in the 16th century left an indelible mark on Panglao, introducing Christianity and leaving behind architectural remnants such as churches and fortifications. In the 20th century, Panglao underwent rapid modernization with the development of tourism infrastructure, attracting visitors from around the globe. Today, Panglao stands as a testament to its diverse heritage, with its pristine beaches, centuries-old churches, and vibrant culture attracting tourists seeking both relaxation and a glimpse into its storied past. As Panglao continues to evolve, it remains a beacon of resilience and cultural richness in the Philippines.
